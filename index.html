About Nitya

Nitya was born on March 7, 2000. I first met her in high school, although before we even became friends it was clear that she'd be one of the most passionate, inspiring, and curious people I'd ever meet. She's an amazing lifelong learner and teacher. In high school, she excelled in her courses while also mentoring others in her extracurriculars. She was a programming mentor in the Athena Robotics club and she led Sectionals (section practices) in the school's orchestra as the second chair second violin. For college, she moved on to graduate from UCLA with two Bachelor of Science degrees: Applied Mathematics and Astrophysics. Now she is currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Rochester, and she has been continously recognized for her dedication to her studies and mentorship throughout this time.

Major Successes

After all of her awesome efforts in research and mentorship, Nitya has received several awards and recognitions. Below I've outlined some of her many notable successes, mostly focused on the strides she's made academically.

  • Graduate Research Assistant at the University of Rochester Department of Physics and Astronomy
    • Modeling total mass and stellar mass rotation curves for disk galaxies in SDSS MaNGA catalog
    • Calculating individual mass components for galaxies and statistical analysis of galaxy mass components and observable galaxy properties
    • Received award for most outstanding graduate teaching assistant
  • Published a paper about the visible mass in SDSS MaNGA Disk Galaxies as the head author
    • Presented a talk and created an e-poster for this work
  • Is an early Ph.D candidate! I suppose it's not an achievement that "contributes to the general public" but since she's a second-year in the Ph.D program and the whole process to go from a Ph.D student to candidate is usually accomplished in later years, I wanted to share this. It's supremely epic and really speaks to her passion and knowledge!
  • Undergraduate Research Assistant at UCLA Department of Astronomy
  • Research Intern at NASA Ames Research Center

Conclusion

Nitya has and will undoubtedly make great contributions to the fields of Physics and Astronomy, and to the world in general. However, the qualities that I've come to know her for were the reasons why I chose her as my tribute. She's incredibly tenacious, driven, and humble in her work. With others, she's amazingly thoughtful, kind, dependable, and fun. Having her in my life has only been joy after joy, and I'm so grateful to be able to share such a great, inspiring person with the the world!!

"She's supremely awesome and, most importantly, my best friend!"

— T.K. Bui
